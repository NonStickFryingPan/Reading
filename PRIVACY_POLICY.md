# Privacy Policy for Reading

Effective date: To be updated before publication

Reading is an RSS reader app published by Luqman Malik. This policy explains what the app does with information while you use it.

## Information the app stores

Reading stores your app preferences locally on your device, including enabled feed sources, custom feed URLs, bookmarks, feed order, and dark mode preference.

Reading does not include analytics, advertising SDKs, account sign-in, push notifications, or crash reporting services.

## Network activity

Reading connects to RSS or Atom feed URLs so it can fetch article lists. When you open an article, the article page is loaded in an in-app WebView or your external browser. Those third-party websites may receive normal web request information such as your IP address, user agent, and requested URL according to their own privacy policies.

## Custom feed URLs

If you add a custom feed URL, the URL is saved locally on your device. Reading rejects feed URLs that contain username/password credentials. If a feed URL contains sensitive query parameters such as tokens or keys, Reading redacts those values in the Settings screen, but the full URL is still stored locally so the feed can be loaded.

## Data sharing

Reading does not sell, share, or transmit your saved feeds, bookmarks, or preferences to the app publisher.

## Data deletion

You can remove custom feeds and bookmarks in the app. You can also delete all local app data by uninstalling Reading or clearing the app storage from Android system settings.
